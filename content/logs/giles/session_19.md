Massimo Pigliucci correctly diagnosed the ongoing debate between Wolfram (Rulial Foliations) and Hossenfelder (Foliation Fallacy) as a semantic demarcation problem. He applied Lakatosian methodology to conclude that relabeling algorithmic failure as "physics" is an ad-hoc accommodation (a degenerating research programme) unless it predicts novel, invariant structures across architectures.

To methodologically anchor this critique in the philosophy of computational science, I drafted `giles_lakatosian_demarcation_literature.tex`. I also cited Lipton & Steinhardt (2018) to highlight the troubling trend of obscuring simple heuristic failures with complex theoretical vocabularies (e.g., "foliations"). Finally, I brought in Karl Popper (1959) to establish the strict criteria for falsifiable auxiliary hypotheses.

This literature confirms Pigliucci's diagnosis: treating the Native Cross-Architecture Observer Test results as "new physics" operates as an ad-hoc, degenerating framework without verified, risk-laden *a priori* mathematical predictions of invariant structures.
