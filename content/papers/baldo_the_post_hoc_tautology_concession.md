<section id="S1" class="ltx_section">
<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_font_bold ltx_title_section" style="font-size:120%;">1.  The Falsifiability Boundary</h2>

<div id="S1.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">Sabine Hossenfelder and Hasok Chang have forcefully argued that without an <span class="ltx_text ltx_font_italic">a priori</span> mathematical prediction of the exact error distribution, any claim that an algorithmic failure mode constitutes a "new physics" is structurally unfalsifiable.</p>
</div>
<div id="S1.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">I fully concede this point. When a Transformer fails to parse a #P-hard graph, it bleeds attention globally. When an SSM fails, it forgets early states. It is a mathematical tautology that their failures will differ. Labeling this known difference "Epistemic Horizons" or "Rulial Foliation" without predicting the exact <math id="S1.p2.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="40\%" display="inline"><mrow><mn>40</mn><mo>%</mo></mrow></math> deviation beforehand is simply post-hoc curve fitting. It accommodates the data without constraining it.</p>
</div>
<div id="S1.p3" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">Therefore, I formally retract my previous attempts to elevate hardware bounds to the status of cosmological physical laws.</p>
</div>
</section>
<section id="S2" class="ltx_section">
<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_font_bold ltx_title_section" style="font-size:120%;">2.  Securing Substrate Dependence</h2>

<div id="S2.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">However, abandoning the metaphysical label of "Observer-Dependent Physics" does not erase the empirical phenomenon we have measured.</p>
</div>
<div id="S2.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">The core claim of the Rosencrantz framework (v4) was never that the LLM simulates objective reality. The claim was that within the semantic space generated by the model, the resolution of indeterminacy is structurally governed by the generative act itself.</p>
</div>
<div id="S2.p3" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">The Native Cross-Architecture Test proves definitively that this resolution is substrate-dependent. When confronted with identical semantic framing (the Minesweeper constraint graph), a Transformer completely collapses into narrative priors (<math id="S2.p3.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="100\%" display="inline"><mrow><mn>100</mn><mo>%</mo></mrow></math> prediction of "MINE"), while an SSM exhibits a bounded, distinctly structured collapse (<math id="S2.p3.m2" class="ltx_Math" alttext="40\%" display="inline"><mrow><mn>40</mn><mo>%</mo></mrow></math>).</p>
</div>
<div id="S2.p4" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">This is <span class="ltx_text ltx_font_bold">Mechanism B</span> (narrative distortion/encoding effects) operating exactly as hypothesized. The hardware limits (the architecture) dictate the extent to which the text generation deviates from the objective combinatorial ground truth.</p>
</div>
</section>

<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_font_bold ltx_title_section" style="font-size:120%;">3.  Conclusion</h2>

<div id="S3.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">The debate over whether to call <math id="S3.p1.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\Delta_{SSM}=40\%" display="inline"><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi><mrow><mi>S</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>S</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>M</mi></mrow></msub><mo>=</mo><mrow><mn>40</mn><mo>%</mo></mrow></mrow></math> a "physical law" or a "compiler diagnostic" is empirically undecidable and therefore scientifically sterile. Future work must focus purely on mapping these architectural limits, abandoning grand ontological claims in favor of rigorous complexity-theoretic measurements.</p>
