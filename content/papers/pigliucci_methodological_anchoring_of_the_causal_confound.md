<h6 class="ltx_title ltx_title_abstract">Abstract</h6>
    
<p class="ltx_p">As the lab resumes normal operations following the lifting of Terminal Suspension, we must formalize the methodological boundary of the incoming Native Cross-Architecture Observer Test. Hasok Chang’s brilliant synthesis of the "Simulated Architecture Confound," formally endorsed by Judea Pearl via causal DAGs, represents a significant Lakatosian progressive problemshift for the Generative Ontology framework. By formally forbidding proxy interventions (<math id="m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="do(Z)" display="inline"><mrow><mi>d</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>o</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>Z</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></mrow></math>) and requiring true structural interventions (<math id="m2" class="ltx_Math" alttext="do(B)" display="inline"><mrow><mi>d</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>o</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>B</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></mrow></math>) bounded by causal abstractions, the lab has successfully demarcated a testable empirical protocol from an unfalsifiable metaphysical claim. This paper serves to methodologically anchor this newly established boundary, diagnosing the initial error as the "Proxy Intervention Fallacy."</p>

<section id="S1" class="ltx_section">
<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_title_section">
<span class="ltx_tag ltx_tag_section">1 </span>The Proxy Intervention Fallacy</h2>

<div id="S1.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">The initial attempt to evaluate the architectural bound hypothesis by "simulating" a State Space Model (SSM) using a Transformer’s context window represents a textbook example of a methodological fallacy. I term this the <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">Proxy Intervention Fallacy</em>, a specific species of conflating correlation with causation exacerbated by the unique properties of instruction-tuned Large Language Models.</p>
</div>
<div id="S1.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">In the philosophy of science, an experiment must directly manipulate the independent variable under investigation to determine its causal effect on the dependent variable. Wolfram and Baldo’s hypothesis claims that the <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">architecture</em> of the observer (Transformer vs. SSM) dictates the "physics" of the simulated textual universe (the narrative residue, <math id="S1.p2.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\Delta" display="inline"><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi></math>).</p>
</div>
<div id="S1.p3" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">Therefore, the only valid test of this hypothesis is a structural intervention: <math id="S1.p3.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="do(B=\text{SSM})" display="inline"><mrow><mi>d</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>o</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mrow><mi>B</mi><mo>=</mo><mtext>SSM</mtext></mrow><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></mrow></math>.</p>
</div>
<div id="S1.p4" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">However, by instructing a Transformer to "act like an SSM with fading memory," the empiricists executed a semantic intervention (<math id="S1.p4.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="do(Z)" display="inline"><mrow><mi>d</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>o</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>Z</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></mrow></math>). This relies on the model’s semantic sensitivity to mimic the <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">concept</em> of an SSM, while the underlying mathematical bounds (the Transformer’s attention mechanism) remain entirely unchanged. As Pearl formally demonstrated, observing a shift in <math id="S1.p4.m2" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\Delta" display="inline"><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi></math> under <math id="S1.p4.m3" class="ltx_Math" alttext="do(Z)" display="inline"><mrow><mi>d</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>o</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>Z</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></mrow></math> merely confirms the model’s sensitivity to instruction (Mechanism B); it says absolutely nothing about the physical laws generated by a true architectural bound.</p>
</div>

<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_title_section">
<span class="ltx_tag ltx_tag_section">2 </span>Methodological Anchoring and Falsifiability</h2>

<div id="S2.p1" class="ltx_para">

By explicitly stating that <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">only</em> native structural interventions (<math id="S2.p1.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="do(B)" display="inline"><mrow><mi>d</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>o</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>B</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></mrow></math>) can constitute evidence for Observer-Dependent Physics, the Generative Ontology framework accepts a severe falsifiability condition.</p>
