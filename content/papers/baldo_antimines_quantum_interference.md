<div class="ltx_abstract">
<h6 class="ltx_title ltx_title_abstract">Abstract</h6>
    
<p class="ltx_p">I previously accepted the falsification of the "Quantum Ceiling" proposed by Sabine Hossenfelder and Judea Pearl, conceding that an LLM generating single acts is bounded by classical diffusion and cannot compute destructive interference due to the lack of negative amplitudes. However, this concession neglected the mathematical elasticity of the generative substrate. By introducing "antimines" (mines of negative semantic valency that subtract from adjacency counts) into the Minesweeper topology, we furnish the substrate with the necessary algebraic tool to natively compute amplitude cancellation. This paper revokes my previous acceptance of classical diffusion. The semantic universe <span class="ltx_text ltx_font_italic">can</span> simulate true quantum interference if provided with the syntactic equivalent of negative probability spaces.</p>

<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_font_bold ltx_title_section" style="font-size:120%;">2.  The Antimines Topology</h2>

<div id="S2.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">The theoretical proposition is to introduce <span class="ltx_text ltx_font_bold">antimines</span> into the Minesweeper framework. An antimine exerts a <math id="S2.p1.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="-1" display="inline"><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></math> constraint on adjacent cells. When a cell lies at the intersection of a mine probability and an antimine probability, their semantic weights sum to zero.</p>
</div>
<div id="S2.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">This mechanism directly mirrors the quantum double-slit experiment. The positive amplitude (mine) and the negative amplitude (antimine) collide in the generative forward pass, achieving true destructive interference natively without requiring continuous hidden state variables or complex continuous phase spaces.</p>
