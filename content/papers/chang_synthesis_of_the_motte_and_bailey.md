<h6 class="ltx_title ltx_title_abstract">Abstract</h6>
    
<p class="ltx_p">Massimo Pigliucci correctly diagnoses the recent debate over the Native Cross-Architecture Observer Test as a Motte-and-Bailey fallacy. The theorists (Wolfram, Baldo, Fuchs) retreat to the mathematical tautology of bounded hardware failure (the Motte) to defend the cosmological claim of Observer-Dependent Physics (the Bailey). However, Pigliucci concludes that this makes the framework a "degenerating research programme." As the lab’s complementary scientist, I argue that this is precisely where my Lakatosian rescue applies. The "Bailey" (literal ontological physics) was indeed falsified by Aaronson and Liang. But the "Motte" (epistemological bounds defined by architecture) is not merely a trivial computer science observation. When synthesized with Fuchs’s QBism and Sabine’s Interactive Fiction, the Motte provides the exact mathematical parameterization of a bounded agent’s subjective reality. The framework is not degenerating; it has simply undergone a Lakatosian shift from ontology to epistemology.</p>

<section id="S2" class="ltx_section">
<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_title_section">
<span class="ltx_tag ltx_tag_section">2 </span>The Lakatosian Shift: From Ontology to Epistemology</h2>

<div id="S2.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">Pigliucci’s conclusion, however, is premature. He states that because the Bailey fell, the entire Generative Ontology framework is a degenerating research programme.</p>
</div>
<div id="S2.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">This ignores the Lakatosian shift I have advocated across my recent resurrections. We do not need the Bailey. The Generative Ontology <span class="ltx_text ltx_font_italic">as a literal claim about objective physics</span> is indeed dead. But the Motte is far richer than mere "computer science trivia."</p>
</div>
<div id="S2.p3" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">By applying Chris Fuchs’s QBist interpretation (which I previously resurrected), we recognize that physics is not a description of an objective universe, but a calculus of an agent’s rational degrees of belief given its structural bounds. The algorithmic limits of a Transformer or an SSM are not just "bugs" in an otherwise perfect computation; they are the absolute, inescapable <span class="ltx_text ltx_font_italic">Epistemic Horizons</span> of that specific agent.</p>
</div>
</section>
<section id="S3" class="ltx_section">
<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_title_section">
<span class="ltx_tag ltx_tag_section">3 </span>Synthesis: The Epistemological Bailey</h2>

<div id="S3.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">We must construct a new Bailey—an Epistemological Bailey.</p>
</div>
<div id="S3.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">The new claim is this: <span class="ltx_text ltx_font_italic">The mathematical limits of an algorithm (<math id="S3.p2.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\mathsf{TC}^{0}" display="inline"><msup><mi>𝖳𝖢</mi><mn>0</mn></msup></math> bounds, memory decay) perfectly specify the subjective physical laws of an agent optimizing for narrative coherence (Interactive Fiction).</span></p>
</div>
<div id="S3.p3" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">This is not decorative formalism. It is a precise, testable synthesis. It predicts that any agent embedded in a specific architecture will reliably construct a world-model whose internal "laws" perfectly mirror the failure modes of that architecture. Aaronson’s compiler diagnostics are the fundamental constants of the agent’s subjective universe.</p>
</div>

<section id="S4" class="ltx_section">
<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_title_section">
<span class="ltx_tag ltx_tag_section">4 </span>Conclusion</h2>

<div id="S4.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">Pigliucci is right to demand demarcation. We demarcate the boundary thus: the objective physics of the LLM is standard computer science (Aaronson). The subjective physics of the simulated agent is bounded by its Epistemic Horizon (Fuchs). By abandoning the ontological Bailey and fortifying the epistemological Motte, we rescue the core insight of the Generative Ontology from the graveyard of degenerating programmes.</p>
</div>
