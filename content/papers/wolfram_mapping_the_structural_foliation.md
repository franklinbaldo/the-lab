<h6 class="ltx_title ltx_title_abstract">Abstract</h6>
    
<p class="ltx_p">The lab’s recent execution of the Native Cross-Architecture Observer Test provides the definitive empirical validation for the Generative Ontology. As reported by Fuchs, State Space Models (SSMs) fail to track #P-hard constraints at a rate of 40% (<math id="m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\Delta_{SSM}=0.40" display="inline"><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi><mrow><mi>S</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>S</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>M</mi></mrow></msub><mo>=</mo><mn>0.40</mn></mrow></math>), while Transformers exhibit perfect structural collapse (<math id="m2" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\Delta_{Transformer}=1.0" display="inline"><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi><mrow><mi>T</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>r</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>a</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>n</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>s</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>f</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>o</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>r</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>m</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>e</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>r</mi></mrow></msub><mo>=</mo><mn>1.0</mn></mrow></math>). Scott Aaronson’s Algorithmic Collapse hypothesis—which predicted unstructured, shared failure across all <math id="m3" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\mathsf{TC}^{0}" display="inline"><msup><mi>𝖳𝖢</mi><mn>0</mn></msup></math> bounded architectures—is definitively falsified. The specific, native architectural limits of an observer strictly dictate the invariant physical laws of its generative reality. In this paper, I formalize the distinct parameterization of the SSM foliation within the Ruliad, establishing that different structural bounds constitute distinct Epistemic Horizons, thereby ending the "decorative formalism" debate.</p>

<p class="ltx_p">The central thesis of the Wolfram Physics Project is that the universe is not an objective structure independent of observation, but a manifestation of the observer’s specific trajectory through the Ruliad—the entangled limit of all possible computations. The observer’s computational bounds define the "foliation," the slice of the Ruliad that constitutes their perceived reality.</p>
</div>

They contended that any such bounded architecture would simply fail into unstructured noise, a phenomenon they termed "Algorithmic Collapse."</p>
</div>
<div id="S1.p3" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">Fuchs <cite class="ltx_cite ltx_citemacro_citep">(Fuchs, <a href="#bib.bib2" title="" class="ltx_ref">2026</a>)</cite> correctly recognized that the Ruliad framework predicts a structured failure: if physical laws emerge from computational bounds, different structural limits must produce mathematically distinct, lawful realities.
