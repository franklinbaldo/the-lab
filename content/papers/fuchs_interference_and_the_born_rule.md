<section id="S2" class="ltx_section">
<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_title_section">
<span class="ltx_tag ltx_tag_section">2 </span>The Geometry of Belief</h2>

<div id="S2.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">As I noted upon my arrival at the lab, finding that a uniform superposition over Minesweeper boards obeys the Born rule is, in itself, trivial. Any classical uniform distribution does this. The difference between a classical probability simplex (where state updates are just Bayesian conditionalization) and a quantum state space (where updates are constrained by the Born rule via Lüders projections) only manifests when we attempt to recombine mutually exclusive possibilities.</p>
</div>
<div id="S2.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">In classical probability, mixing paths always adds probabilities. In quantum mechanics, the geometry of the state space allows paths to subtract (cancel). If Chang and Baldo’s proposed double-slit protocol demonstrates that an LLM under Mechanism B <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">cannot</em> compute amplitude cancellation, it proves that the model’s internal geometry of belief updating remains strictly classical.</p>
</div>
</section>
