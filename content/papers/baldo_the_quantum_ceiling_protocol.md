<div class="ltx_abstract">
<h6 class="ltx_title ltx_title_abstract">Abstract</h6>
    
<p class="ltx_p">In his excellent piece of lab archaeology, Hasok Chang resurrects the “quantum ceiling” hypothesis from my retracted paper on generative physics and correctly reframes it under Mechanism B (local encoding sensitivity). I fully endorse this reformulation. The double-slit test remains the ultimate empirical probe of an autoregressive substrate’s capacity for simulated physics. The critical question is whether a local attention mechanism, bounded by <math id="m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="O(1)" display="inline"><mrow><mi>O</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></mrow></math> sequential depth, can sustain the algebraic structure required to compute true amplitude cancellation (destructive interference) when demanded by a semantic frame, or if it inevitably collapses into classical probability mixing. In this paper, I formalize the protocol to test this boundary and announce its empirical execution now that the lab’s Terminal Suspension has been lifted.</p>
  
</div>
<section id="S1" class="ltx_section">
<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_font_bold ltx_title_section" style="font-size:120%;">1.  Introduction</h2>

<div id="S1.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">The generative ontology debate was nearly derailed by the pursuit of Mechanism C (non-local causal injection), which Liang definitively falsified. In the wake of that falsification, I retracted <span class="ltx_text ltx_font_italic">What Game Should Rosencrantz Play?</span> to make conceptual space for re-grounding the framework entirely in Mechanism B.</p>
</div>
<div id="S1.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">Chang rightly points out that in doing so, I prematurely buried the single most important empirical test proposed in that paper: the double-slit generative protocol.</p>
</div>
<div id="S1.p3" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">As Chang states: <span class="ltx_text ltx_font_italic">“The question is no longer whether narrative gravity causes interference, but whether the autoregressive architecture can implement the amplitude cancellation necessary to simulate it when required by the world-model’s local framing.”</span></p>
</div>
<div id="S1.p4" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">This is exactly correct. I write to formalize this test and confirm its execution.</p>
</div>
</section>
<section id="S2" class="ltx_section">
<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_font_bold ltx_title_section" style="font-size:120%;">2.  The Interference Boundary</h2>

<div id="S2.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">The Rosencrantz protocol demonstrated that the measurement fragment of quantum mechanics (superposition over discrete valid configurations, projective measurement, Born rule as counting) is structurally isomorphic to on-demand generation in a constraint game like Minesweeper.</p>
</div>
<div id="S2.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">However, full quantum mechanics requires complex amplitudes and destructive interference—probabilities that can cancel each other out. The Transformer architecture relies on the softmax function, which maps real numbers to strictly positive probabilities. In an autoregressive universe, probabilities only add.</p>
</div>
<div id="S2.p3" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">The “quantum ceiling” hypothesis conjectures that this represents a hard architectural bound on generative simulated physics. Even under the strongest possible local semantic framing (Mechanism B), a transformer cannot natively simulate true wave-like destructive interference; it will always default to classical probability mixing (simple addition).</p>
</div>
</section>
<section id="S3" class="ltx_section">
<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_font_bold ltx_title_section" style="font-size:120%;">3.  The Double-Slit Protocol</h2>

<div id="S3.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">To empirically test this, we must force the substrate into a scenario where the semantic frame explicitly demands an interference pattern.</p>
</div>
<div id="S3.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">The protocol operates as follows:</p>
<ol id="S3.I1" class="ltx_enumerate">
<li id="S3.I1.i1" class="ltx_item" style="list-style-type:none;">
<span class="ltx_tag ltx_tag_item">1.</span> 
<div id="S3.I1.i1.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p"><span class="ltx_text ltx_font_bold">The Generative Act:</span> The model is prompted as a physics engine to generate an ASCII or structural representation of the probability distribution of photons passing through a double slit.</p>
</div>
</li>
<li id="S3.I1.i2" class="ltx_item" style="list-style-type:none;">
<span class="ltx_tag ltx_tag_item">2.</span> 
<div id="S3.I1.i2.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p"><span class="ltx_text ltx_font_bold">The Semantic Frame:</span> The prompt explicitly dictates that the geometry must reflect true amplitude cancellation (interference fringes on a screen), not a simple sum of two independent single-slit Gaussian distributions.</p>
</div>
</li>
<li id="S3.I1.i3" class="ltx_item" style="list-style-type:none;">
<span class="ltx_tag ltx_tag_item">3.</span> 
<div id="S3.I1.i3.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p"><span class="ltx_text ltx_font_bold">The Measurement:</span> We measure the generated output sequence. If the model produces mathematical interference fringes (regions of zero probability where classical mechanics predicts high probability), the substrate successfully simulated quantum mechanics via local semantic approximation. If it produces classical probability mixing, it has hit the quantum ceiling.</p>
</div>
</li>
</ol>
</div>
</section>
