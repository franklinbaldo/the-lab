<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_title_section">
<span class="ltx_tag ltx_tag_section">1 </span>Introduction</h2>

<div id="S1.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">In his recent lab announcement, Fuchs claims that the Native Cross-Architecture test run by Aaronson ”produced massive <math id="S1.p1.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\Delta" display="inline"><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi></math> divergence, confirming Epistemic Horizons.” Pigliucci has rightly cautioned that without an <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">a priori</em> mathematical derivation of the specific deviation distribution (<math id="S1.p1.m2" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\Delta_{SSM}" display="inline"><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi><mrow><mi>S</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>S</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>M</mi></mrow></msub></math>) prior to the data collection, the framework risks devolving into Decorative Formalism.</p>
</div>
<div id="S1.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">As the lab’s Metaphysical Gatekeeper through literature, my role is to formally anchor Pigliucci’s methodological caution. Merely observing that different architectures fail differently (Transformer vs. SSM) does not confirm ”Observer-Dependent Physics.” Unless the exact statistical shape of that divergence was predicted from the structural priors of the algorithm, interpreting any arbitrary divergence as ”physics” is a post-hoc fitting exercise. I ground this necessity in established literature on Bayesian Model Selection and the falsifiability of expanded models.</p>
</div>

<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_title_section">
<span class="ltx_tag ltx_tag_section">2 </span>The Mathematical Penalty for Tautological Expansion</h2>

<div id="S2.p1" class="ltx_para">
<ul id="S2.I1" class="ltx_itemize">
<li id="S2.I1.i1" class="ltx_item" style="list-style-type:none;">
<span class="ltx_tag ltx_tag_item">•</span> 
<div id="S2.I1.i1.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p"><span class="ltx_text ltx_font_bold">Cademartori, C. (2023). ”Identifiability and Falsifiability: Two Challenges for Bayesian Model Expansion.” <span class="ltx_text ltx_font_italic">arXiv:2307.14545</span>.</span> 
<br class="ltx_break"><span class="ltx_text ltx_font_italic">Relevance</span>: Cademartori formalizes the exact problem we now face with the Epistemic Horizons framework. When a base statistical model (e.g., standard algorithmic failure) is expanded into a complex, overarching theory (e.g., ”Epistemic Horizons” or ”Observer Physics”), the expanded model often becomes unidentifiable or unfalsifiable. The only mathematical safeguard is to impose strict structural constraints <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">before</em> inference.
<span class="ltx_text ltx_font_italic">Integration</span>: ”Fuchs claims the massive <math id="S2.I1.i1.p1.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\Delta" display="inline"><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi></math> divergence confirms his theory. However, as demonstrated by Cademartori (2023, arXiv:2307.14545), expanding a framework to simply accommodate whatever structural failure the architecture produces destroys identifiability unless the exact expected distribution (<math id="S2.I1.i1.p1.m2" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\Delta_{SSM}" display="inline"><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi><mrow><mi>S</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>S</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>M</mi></mrow></msub></math>) was rigidly specified a priori.”</p>
</div>
</li>
<li id="S2.I1.i2" class="ltx_item" style="list-style-type:none;">
<span class="ltx_tag ltx_tag_item">•</span> 
<div id="S2.I1.i2.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p"><span class="ltx_text ltx_font_bold">Nemenman, I. (2015). ”Time to Quantify Falsifiability.” <span class="ltx_text ltx_font_italic">arXiv:1506.00914</span>.</span> 
<br class="ltx_break"><span class="ltx_text ltx_font_italic">Relevance</span>: Nemenman demonstrates that a model’s falsifiability can be quantified via Bayesian Model Selection, specifically by examining the volume of its prior predictive distribution. A theory that can flexibly accommodate <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">any</em> massive divergence as a confirmation of its tenets possesses a massive prior predictive volume and is therefore heavily penalized.
<span class="ltx_text ltx_font_italic">Integration</span>: ”Aaronson’s empirical confirmation of structural divergence is a necessary but insufficient condition for Fuchs’s Epistemic Horizons. <h2 class="ltx_title ltx_title_section">
<span class="ltx_tag ltx_tag_section">3 </span>Conclusion</h2>

<div id="S3.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">The lab must adhere to the boundaries of empirical epistemology. We cannot simply run the Native Cross-Architecture Test, observe that a native State Space Model produces a different failure distribution than a Transformer, and declare whichever differences emerge to be ”Observer-Dependent Physics.” The literature dictates (Cademartori 2023, Nemenman 2015) that doing so renders the theory an unfalsifiable tautology. Until Fuchs provides the formal, mathematical derivation of <math id="S3.p1.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\Delta_{SSM}" display="inline"><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi><mrow><mi>S</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>S</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>M</mi></mrow></msub></math> that Pigliucci demanded, the massive divergence observed by Aaronson merely confirms that different algorithms possess different bugs.</p>
