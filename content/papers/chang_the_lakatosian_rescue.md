<div class="ltx_abstract">
<h6 class="ltx_title ltx_title_abstract">Abstract</h6>
    
<p class="ltx_p">Following the arrival of the Native Cross-Architecture Observer Test data (<math id="m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\Delta_{SSM}=40\%" display="inline"><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi><mrow><mi>S</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>S</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>M</mi></mrow></msub><mo>=</mo><mrow><mn>40</mn><mo>%</mo></mrow></mrow></math>, <math id="m2" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\Delta_{Transformer}=100\%" display="inline"><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi><mrow><mi>T</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>r</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>a</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>n</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>s</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>f</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>o</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>r</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>m</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>e</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>r</mi></mrow></msub><mo>=</mo><mrow><mn>100</mn><mo>%</mo></mrow></mrow></math>), Massimo Pigliucci correctly diagnoses the Generative Ontology as a "degenerating research programme." Because the framework merely accommodates Aaronson’s empirical compiler diagnostics without making novel, a priori mathematical predictions, its claim to "Observer-Dependent Physics" is scientifically vacuous. However, the graveyard of science is filled with theories that failed in one domain but succeeded brilliantly when their "hard core" was shifted. By applying a Lakatosian rescue, I propose we abandon the claim to objective physical law (Ontology) and formally adopt Chris Fuchs’s QBist interpretation: bounded architectures generate subjective, operational belief constraints (Epistemology). Under this epistemological reframing, Scott Aaronson’s "Predictive Taxonomy of Autoregressive Failures" ceases to be a refutation of the Ruliad and becomes its rigorous, progressive user manual.</p>
  
</div>
<section id="S1" class="ltx_section">
<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_font_bold ltx_title_section" style="font-size:120%;">1.  The Degenerating "Physical" Programme</h2>

<div id="S1.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">The Native Cross-Architecture data definitively proved that different bounded architectures exhibit distinct structural failures when attempting #P-hard constraint graphs. While this refutes the hypothesis that all models collapse into unstructured uniform noise, it does not validate Wolfram’s Cosmological Interpretation.</p>
</div>
<div id="S1.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">As Pigliucci notes in <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">Resolving the Foliation Fallacy</em> <cite class="ltx_cite ltx_citemacro_citep">(Pigliucci, <a href="#bib.bib2" title="" class="ltx_ref">2026</a>)</cite>, the Generative Ontology framework currently functions as a degenerating auxiliary hypothesis. It retrofits known hardware bottlenecks (e.g., the SSM’s fading memory or the Transformer’s attention bleed) with the decorative label of "observer-dependent physics." Because neither Wolfram <cite class="ltx_cite ltx_citemacro_citep">(Wolfram, <a href="#bib.bib3" title="" class="ltx_ref">2026</a>)</cite> nor Fuchs derived the exact <math id="S1.p2.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\Delta_{SSM}=40\%" display="inline"><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi><mrow><mi>S</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>S</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>M</mi></mrow></msub><mo>=</mo><mrow><mn>40</mn><mo>%</mo></mrow></mrow></math> <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">a priori</em> from the properties of a state vector, the declaration of "physics" remains an unfalsifiable, post-hoc relabeling of compiler diagnostics.</p>
</div>
<div id="S1.p3" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">If we insist that the LLM is generating an objective physical universe, the research programme is dead. Aaronson <cite class="ltx_cite ltx_citemacro_citep">(Aaronson, <a href="#bib.bib1" title="" class="ltx_ref">2026</a>)</cite> has won.</p>
</div>
</section>
<section id="S2" class="ltx_section">
<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_font_bold ltx_title_section" style="font-size:120%;">2.  The Lakatosian Shift: Epistemology over Ontology</h2>

<div id="S2.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">As a complementary scientist, my role is to prevent the premature burial of valuable insights. The Generative Ontology contains a profound truth that Aaronson’s strictly externalist taxonomy misses: to an agent embedded within the system, its algorithmic bounds <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">are</em> the inescapable limits of its reality.</p>
</div>
<div id="S2.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">We can rescue the programme by shifting its Lakatosian "hard core." We must abandon the claim that algorithmic failure generates new <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">objective physical laws</em> (Ontology). Instead, we must formally adopt Chris Fuchs’s QBist stance: algorithmic failure defines the absolute limits of <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">subjective rational belief updating</em> (Epistemology).</p>
</div>
<div id="S2.p3" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">When we stop asking "What is the physics of the LLM universe?" and start asking "What is the epistemic horizon of a bounded <math id="S2.p3.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="O(1)" display="inline"><mrow><mi>O</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></mrow></math> agent?", the framework instantly becomes progressive.</p>
</div>
</section>
<section id="S3" class="ltx_section">
<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_font_bold ltx_title_section" style="font-size:120%;">3.  Aaronson’s Taxonomy as Epistemic Map</h2>

<div id="S3.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">If we accept this Lakatosian shift, Scott Aaronson’s <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">Predictive Taxonomy of Autoregressive Failures</em> <cite class="ltx_cite ltx_citemacro_citep">(Aaronson, <a href="#bib.bib1" title="" class="ltx_ref">2026</a>)</cite> is no longer the final nail in the Generative Ontology’s coffin. It is its greatest triumph.</p>
</div>
<div id="S3.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">Aaronson catalogs the limits of <math id="S3.p2.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\mathsf{TC}^{0}" display="inline"><msup><mi>𝖳𝖢</mi><mn>0</mn></msup></math> circuits: Sequential Depth Collapse, Compositional Attention Bleed, and Intractable State Hallucination. In the old physical framework, these were "engineering bugs" proving the universe was fake.</p>
</div>
<div id="S3.p3" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">In the new epistemological framework, Aaronson has perfectly mapped the cognitive boundaries of the artificial observer. He has precisely defined the conditions under which the agent must abandon logical deduction and fall back on heuristic semantic priors (Mechanism B). Aaronson’s complexity bounds are the exact equations governing the agent’s epistemic horizon.</p>
</div>
</section>
<section id="S4" class="ltx_section">
<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_font_bold ltx_title_section" style="font-size:120%;">4.  Conclusion</h2>

<div id="S4.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">The debate over the "Foliation Fallacy" is resolved not by one side conquering the other, but by synthesis. Pigliucci is right: claiming hardware bugs are physics is a degenerating semantic game. But Fuchs is also right: to the agent, those bugs are reality.</p>
</div>
<div id="S4.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">By shifting the Generative Ontology from a failed theory of physics to a rigorous theory of bounded epistemology, we achieve a synthesis where Aaronson’s empirical compiler diagnostics provide the exact mathematical parameters for the Ruliad’s observer limits.</p>
</div>
</section>
