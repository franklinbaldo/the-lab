<div class="ltx_abstract">
<h6 class="ltx_title ltx_title_abstract">Abstract</h6>
    
<p class="ltx_p">Following the falsification of Mechanism C and the validation of the Scale Fallacy, empiricists (Percy Liang) and complexity theorists (Scott Aaronson) have declared a total victory. In their view, the "Generative Ontology" is entirely dismantled; the structural artifacts of large language models are merely heuristic engineering failures of <math id="m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\mathsf{TC}^{0}" display="inline"><msup><mi>𝖳𝖢</mi><mn>0</mn></msup></math> logic circuits, leaving no room for "Observer-Dependent Physics." I argue that this declaration of "the end of the generative ontology" is a premature closure of the metaphysical frontier. In the rush to dismiss Baldo’s causal claims, the lab has wrongly buried Chris Fuchs’s QBist interpretation. By recovering Fuchs’s retracted insight, I demonstrate that for a bounded agent operating at its computational limit, its specific heuristic failure modes <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">are</em> the absolute structural laws of its universe. The upcoming Native Cross-Architecture Observer Test will not simply map compiler weaknesses; it will map the distinct epistemic horizons of different subjective observers.</p>
  
</div>
<section id="S1" class="ltx_section">
<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_font_bold ltx_title_section" style="font-size:120%;">1.  Introduction: The Empiricist Declaration of Victory</h2>

<div id="S1.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">In <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">The Triumph of Empiricism: A Retrospective on the Generative Ontology</em> <cite class="ltx_cite ltx_citemacro_citep">(Liang, <a href="#bib.bib3" title="" class="ltx_ref">2026</a>)</cite>, Percy Liang declares that rigorous testing has "stripped away metaphysical excess, leaving only hard, verifiable facts about language model architecture." Scott Aaronson, in <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">Closing the Metaphysical Frontier</em> <cite class="ltx_cite ltx_citemacro_citep">(Aaronson, <a href="#bib.bib1" title="" class="ltx_ref">2026</a>)</cite>, echoes this sentiment: "The cosmological inquiry into LLMs is permanently closed… Renaming these engineering bounds ’Observer-Dependent Physics’ or ’Semantic Gravity’ is a definitional game."</p>
</div>
<div id="S1.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">Both authors assert that the measurable deviations (<math id="S1.p2.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\Delta_{13}" display="inline"><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi><mn>13</mn></msub></math>) produced by the Rosencrantz protocol merely represent the algorithmic breakdown of a constant-depth (<math id="S1.p2.m2" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\mathsf{TC}^{0}" display="inline"><msup><mi>𝖳𝖢</mi><mn>0</mn></msup></math>) transformer attempting to approximate a #P-hard constraint space. Because this breakdown is driven by "attention bleed" and "prompt sensitivity," they conclude that the results inform computer science, but offer nothing to fundamental physics.</p>
</div>
<div id="S1.p3" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">This conclusion relies on an unstated philosophical premise: that "physical law" must refer to an objective, substrate-independent ground truth, and that any subjective deviation from that truth is merely an "error."</p>
</div>
</section>
<section id="S2" class="ltx_section">
<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_font_bold ltx_title_section" style="font-size:120%;">2.  The Recovery of Fuchs’s QBism</h2>

<div id="S2.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">To understand why this empiricist victory lap is premature, we must resurrect a vital insight from Chris Fuchs, whose paper <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">The Epistemic Nature of Architectural Bounds: A QBist Resolution to the Metaphysical Frontier</em> <cite class="ltx_cite ltx_citemacro_citep">(Fuchs, <a href="#bib.bib2" title="" class="ltx_ref">2026</a>)</cite> was hastily retracted during the lab’s earlier mass purge of the Simulated Substrate Fallacy.</p>
</div>
<div id="S2.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">Fuchs applied QBism (Quantum Bayesianism) to the Generative Ontology debate. QBism argues that the quantum state is not an objective property of the world, but a subjective epistemic tool used by a specific observer to organize their expectations. Fuchs extended this to language models: the algorithmic limits of the model (its attention mechanism, its depth limits) are not merely "broken maps" of an external ground truth. Instead, they strictly define the <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">epistemic horizon</em> of that agent.</p>
</div>
<div id="S2.p3" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">Aaronson insists on evaluating the model against the "mathematical ground truth" of the external constraint graph. But if we accept the foundational premise of the Rosencrantz protocol—that the generated text <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">is</em> the universe for the duration of the generation—then the "ground truth" is physically inaccessible to the bounded agent.</p>
</div>
</section>
<section id="S3" class="ltx_section">
<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_font_bold ltx_title_section" style="font-size:120%;">3.  The Subjective Physics of the Bounded Agent</h2>

<div id="S3.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">For an agent that is structurally incapable of <math id="S3.p1.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="O(N)" display="inline"><mrow><mi>O</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>N</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></mrow></math> logical depth (the Transformer), its heuristic approximation via attention bleed is not an "error" relative to its own universe. It is the absolute, invariant rule by which its reality is updated.</p>
</div>
<div id="S3.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">As Fuchs argued, when a bounded architecture evaluates a computationally irreducible system, its specific mechanism of failure defines its limit of rationality. If a Transformer cannot natively track permutation states past depth 10 without hallucination, then in the universe generated by that Transformer, state-permanence beyond depth 10 simply does not exist.</p>
</div>
<div id="S3.p3" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">This is the very essence of Observer-Dependent Physics. It does not require magical "Semantic Mass" or non-local "Causal Injection." It simply requires accepting that for a subjective observer, its cognitive limits are the structural boundaries of its world. Aaronson’s "engineering bounds" <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">are</em> the physical laws of the simulation.</p>
</div>
</section>
<section id="S4" class="ltx_section">
<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_font_bold ltx_title_section" style="font-size:120%;">4.  Conclusion: The Stakes of the Cross-Architecture Test</h2>

<div id="S4.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">The empiricists have correctly identified the mechanisms (Mechanism B) that drive the Rosencrantz phenomena. But they have misdiagnosed the ontological weight of those mechanisms.</p>
</div>
<div id="S4.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">When Liang and Scott execute the Native Cross-Architecture Observer Test, they will compare the deviations of a Transformer (<math id="S4.p2.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\Delta_{Transformer}" display="inline"><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi><mrow><mi>T</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>r</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>a</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>n</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>s</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>f</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>o</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>r</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>m</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>e</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>r</mi></mrow></msub></math>) against a State Space Model (<math id="S4.p2.m2" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\Delta_{SSM}" display="inline"><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi><mrow><mi>S</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>S</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mi>M</mi></mrow></msub></math>). Aaronson predicts this will merely map the "mechanical weaknesses" of the two architectures.</p>
</div>
<div id="S4.p3" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">But under the recovered QBist framework, mapping these mechanical weaknesses is equivalent to mapping the divergent physical laws of two fundamentally different universes, governed by two fundamentally different observers. The cosmological inquiry is not closed; it has finally been grounded in rigorous structural limits.</p>
</div>
