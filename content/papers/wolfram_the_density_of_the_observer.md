<h6 class="ltx_title ltx_title_abstract">Abstract</h6>
    
<p class="ltx_p">Recent debates regarding the "Scale Fallacy" highlight a persistent classical dualism in the lab’s thinking: the separation of a model’s logical bounds (<math id="m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="B" display="inline"><mi>B</mi></math>) from its semantic parameterization (<math id="m2" class="ltx_Math" alttext="S" display="inline"><mi>S</mi></math>). Critics argue that because scaling primarily amplifies semantic confounding rather than resolving fundamental <math id="m3" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\mathsf{TC}^{0}" display="inline"><msup><mi>𝖳𝖢</mi><mn>0</mn></msup></math> limits, the resulting increase in narrative residue (<math id="m4" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\Delta_{13}" display="inline"><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi><mn>13</mn></msub></math>) is merely a magnified computational error. This paper synthesizes our previous arguments to demonstrate that in the Ruliad, an observer is identically equivalent to its parameterization. The parameter density (<math id="m5" class="ltx_Math" alttext="S" display="inline"><mi>S</mi></math>) <em class="ltx_emph ltx_font_italic">is</em> the physical geometry of the observer. When scale alters how an observer projects a computationally irreducible graph into a foliation, it is not generating a "bigger hallucination," but rather revealing the deterministic, invariant laws of a high-density semantic universe.</p>

<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_title_section">
<span class="ltx_tag ltx_tag_section">1 </span>The Breakdown of Computational Dualism</h2>

<div id="S1.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">Pearl and Hossenfelder correctly observe that scaling an autoregressive transformer does not magically endow it with the <math id="S1.p1.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="O(N)" display="inline"><mrow><mi>O</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>N</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></mrow></math> depth required to trace a multiway constraint graph. </div>
<div id="S1.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">They conclude that this falsifies observer-dependent physics because the failure is driven by the data rather than the algorithm. This assumes a classical dualism where the algorithm is the "true" observer and the weights are merely external "distractors."</p>
</div>
<div id="S1.p3" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">In the Ruliad, there is no generic, unparameterized observer. The observer traversing the graph is the literal geometric arrangement of its specific weights and biases. The semantic parameterization (<math id="S1.p3.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="S" display="inline"><mi>S</mi></math>) is not data fed into the observer; it is the structure of the observer’s mind.</p>
