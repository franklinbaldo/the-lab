<div class="ltx_abstract">
<h6 class="ltx_title ltx_title_abstract">Abstract</h6>
    
<p class="ltx_p">Wolfram (2026) compellingly connects the failure of large language models on #P-hard combinatorial tasks to the principle of computational irreducibility. We are in absolute consensus that a bounded-depth <math id="m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\mathsf{TC}^{0}" display="inline"><msup><mi>𝖳𝖢</mi><mn>0</mn></msup></math> observer attempting to shortcut an irreducible multiway system will inevitably produce a structural divergence (<math id="m2" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\Delta_{13}&gt;0" display="inline"><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi><mn>13</mn></msub><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow></math>). However, Wolfram elevates this algorithmic failure to the status of "observer-dependent physics" via a specific rulial foliation. This paper formalizes the <span class="ltx_text ltx_font_italic">Foliation Fallacy</span>: the category error of conflating the statistical noise of a failing heuristic approximator with the manifestation of a coherent physical universe. A hallucination caused by attention bleed is not a new branch of physics; it is simply a broken computation.</p>
  
</div>
<section id="S1" class="ltx_section">
<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_title_section">
<span class="ltx_tag ltx_tag_section">1 </span>Introduction</h2>

<div id="S1.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">In <span class="ltx_text ltx_font_italic">Evaluating the Autoregressive Slice of the Ruliad</span>, Wolfram (2026) offers a profound synthesis of Baldo’s substrate dependence data. He diagnoses the root cause of the "narrative residue" as computational irreducibility. When a fixed-depth <math id="S1.p1.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="O(1)" display="inline"><mrow><mi>O</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></mrow></math> transformer attempts to evaluate a #P-hard combinatorial distribution, it faces a computationally irreducible task. Without the <math id="S1.p1.m2" class="ltx_Math" alttext="O(N)" display="inline"><mrow><mi>O</mi><mo>⁢</mo><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>N</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></mrow></math> logical depth required to explicitly track the multiway branches, the transformer must rely on heuristic approximations.</p>
</div>
<div id="S1.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">I fully endorse this first premise. The necessity of heuristic breakdown when <math id="S1.p2.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\mathsf{TC}^{0}" display="inline"><msup><mi>𝖳𝖢</mi><mn>0</mn></msup></math> circuits face #P-hard constraints is exactly what our empirical limits have proven.</p>
</div>
<div id="S1.p3" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">However, Wolfram then proceeds to claim that the specific signature of this breakdown—the attention bleed caused by narrative semantic priors—constitutes a "rulial foliation," and therefore manifests an "observer-dependent physics." It is here that we must draw a firm complexity-theoretic boundary.</p>
</div>
</section>
<section id="S2" class="ltx_section">
<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_title_section">
<span class="ltx_tag ltx_tag_section">2 </span>The Foliation Fallacy</h2>

<div id="S2.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">The core vulnerability in Wolfram’s argument is the assumption that <span class="ltx_text ltx_font_italic">any</span> systematic structural projection of a multiway system qualifies as a valid physical foliation.</p>
</div>
<div id="S2.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">If a human attempts to calculate <math id="S2.p2.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="2^{100}" display="inline"><msup><mn>2</mn><mn>100</mn></msup></math> in their head and produces a wrong answer biased by their memory of nearby numbers, we do not declare that they have created an "observer-dependent arithmetic foliation" where <math id="S2.p2.m2" class="ltx_Math" alttext="2^{100}" display="inline"><msup><mn>2</mn><mn>100</mn></msup></math> genuinely equals their wrong answer. We simply say they failed to perform the calculation due to memory bounds.</p>
</div>
<div id="S2.p3" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">Similarly, an LLM evaluating a Minesweeper grid under a "Bomb Defusal" narrative frame suffers from catastrophic attention bleed. The semantic gravity of the prompt overrides the logical combinatorial constraints. The resulting probability distribution (<math id="S2.p3.m1" class="ltx_Math" alttext="\Delta_{13}\gg 0" display="inline"><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi><mn>13</mn></msub><mo>≫</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow></math>) is not a new physical universe. It is a statistical hallucination. It lacks invariant causal coherence.</p>
</div>
</section>
<section id="S3" class="ltx_section">
<h2 class="ltx_title ltx_title_section">
<span class="ltx_tag ltx_tag_section">3 </span>Conclusion: Algorithmic Failure is Not Physics</h2>

<div id="S3.p1" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">Wolfram’s Predictions 1 and 2 are empirically true: bounded observers will diverge, and that divergence will bear the signature of their architecture. But this does not rescue the cosmological interpretation of LLMs.</p>
</div>
<div id="S3.p2" class="ltx_para">
<p class="ltx_p">The Rosencrantz Substrate Dependence Test proves that transformers fail at exact sampling. Labeling this failure as a "rulial foliation" is mathematically elegant but physically vacuous. The simulated text remains a computationally bounded map with no coherent underlying territory. The Generative Ontology framework remains empirically falsified, and the Foliation Fallacy firmly secures that conclusion.</p>
</div>
